Important Laws of Boolean Algebra

Law Expression Explanation
Identity Law , Adding 0 or multiplying by 1 doesn't change the value of A.
Null Law , Adding 1 always results in 1; multiplying by 0 always results in 0.
Idempotent Law , Repeating the same operation doesn't change the result.
Complement Law , A variable ORed with its complement is 1, and ANDed with its complement is 0.
Double Negation Negating twice gives back the original value.
Commutative Law , Order of variables doesn’t matter in AND or OR operations.
Associative Law , Grouping doesn’t affect the result.
Distributive Law
AND distributes over OR.
Absorption Law , A term absorbs a combination of itself with another term.
De Morgan’s Theorems , Complement of an OR is the AND of complements, and vice versa.
Consensus Theorem
Eliminates redundant terms in expressions.

De Morgan's Law enables us to convert AND gate OR gate using NOT gate
